## Unit 1: 자기소개 — Self-Introductions

---

### Pattern: 이에요 / 예요

**Form:** Noun + 이에요 (noun ends in consonant) / Noun + 예요 (noun ends in vowel)

**Meaning:** "is / am / are" — a predicative or interrogative ending attached to a noun. Used in informal polite speech.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 타완이에요. | I am Tawan. |
| 태국 사람이에요? | Are you Thai? |
| 네, 중국 사람이에요. | Yes, I am Chinese. |
| 저는 왕치엔이에요. | I am Wang Qian. |
| 저는 의사예요. | I am a doctor. |

> **Tip:** Use 이에요 after a consonant (학생이에요), and 예요 after a vowel (의사예요).

---

### Pattern: 은 / 는

**Form:** Noun + 은 (noun ends in consonant) / Noun + 는 (noun ends in vowel)

**Meaning:** Topic particle — marks the topic of the sentence. Equivalent to "as for ___" or simply placed after the subject in English.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 타완 씨는 회사원이에요? | Is Tawan a company employee? |
| 네, 저는 회사원이에요. | Yes, I am a company employee. |
| 토야 씨는 몽골 사람이에요. | Toya is Mongolian. |
| 선생님은 한국 사람이에요. | The teacher is Korean. |

> **Tip:** 은/는 indicates the topic of the sentence and can imply contrast (unlike 이/가 which marks the grammatical subject).

## Unit 2: 일상생활 — Daily Activities

---

### Pattern: ~아/어요

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 아요 (if last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ) / 어요 (all other vowels). For 하다 verbs → 해요.

**Meaning:** Present tense polite ending — used to describe actions or states, and to ask questions. The standard informal polite form used in everyday conversation.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 지금 뭐 해요? | What are you doing now? |
| 일해요. | I'm working. |
| 유키 씨는 전화해요. | Yuki is making a phone call. |
| 치엔 씨는 이야기해요. | Chien is talking. |
| 운동해요. | I'm exercising. |

> **Tip:** 하다 is the most common verb and always becomes 해요.

---

### Pattern: 에 가다

**Form:** Place noun + 에 가다

**Meaning:** "go to (a place)" — 에 marks the destination. Used with movement verbs like 가다 (to go), 오다 (to come).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 어디에 가요? | Where are you going? |
| 식당에 가요. | I'm going to the restaurant. |
| 타완 씨는 오늘 학교에 가요. | Tawan is going to school today. |
| 로라 씨는 집에 가요. | Lora is going home. |

## Unit 3: 위치 — Locations and Positions

---

### Pattern: 이 / 가

**Form:** Noun + 이 (noun ends in consonant) / Noun + 가 (noun ends in vowel)

**Meaning:** Subject particle — marks the grammatical subject of the sentence.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 펜이 있어요? | Is there a pen? |
| 네, 펜이 있어요. | Yes, there is a pen. |
| 유키 씨가 있어요? | Is Yuki here? |
| 무엇이 있어요? | What is there? |

---

### Pattern: 에 있다 / 없다

**Form:** Place noun + 에 있다 / 없다

**Meaning:** "to be at (a place) / to not be at (a place)" — indicates the location or existence of a person or object.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 가방이 어디에 있어요? | Where is the bag? |
| 침대 위에 있어요. | It's on the bed. |
| 투이 씨가 집에 있어요? | Is Tui at home? |
| 아니요, 집에 없어요. 학교에 있어요. | No, she's not at home. She's at school. |

## Unit 4: 물건 사기 1 — Shopping 1

---

### Pattern: 을 / 를

**Form:** Noun + 을 (noun ends in consonant) / Noun + 를 (noun ends in vowel)

**Meaning:** Object particle — marks the direct object of a verb (the thing receiving the action).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 마크 씨는 무엇을 사요? | What is Mark buying? |
| 저는 우유를 사요. | I am buying milk. |
| 유키 씨는 무엇을 해요? | What is Yuki doing? |
| 저는 책을 읽어요. | I am reading a book. |

---

### Pattern: (으)세요

**Form:** Verb stem + 으세요 (stem ends in consonant) / 세요 (stem ends in vowel)

**Meaning:** Honorific imperative or polite request — "please do ___". Used when asking or telling someone to do something politely.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 사과 주세요. | Please give me the apples. |
| 책을 읽으세요. | Please read the book. |
| 한국어를 공부하세요. | Please study Korean. |
| 여기 앉으세요. | Please sit here. |

---

## Unit 5: 물건 사기 2 — Shopping 2

---

### Pattern: -ㅂ니다 / 습니다 and -ㅂ니까 / 습니까

**Form:**
- Statement: Verb/Adj stem + ㅂ니다 (stem ends in vowel) / 습니다 (stem ends in consonant)
- Question: Verb/Adj stem + ㅂ니까 (stem ends in vowel) / 습니까 (stem ends in consonant)

**Meaning:** Formal polite ending — used in formal situations, presentations, announcements, and with people of higher status. More formal than ~아/어요.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 무엇을 삽니까? | What will you buy? |
| 주스 하나를 삽니다. | I will buy one juice. |
| 안녕하십니까? | How do you do? (formal greeting) |
| 저는 유키입니다. 반갑습니다. | I am Yuki. Pleased to meet you. |
| 네, 여기 있습니다. | Yes, here it is. |

---

### Pattern: 하고

**Form:** Noun + 하고 + Noun (listing) / Noun + 하고 같이 (together with)

**Meaning:** "and" (for listing nouns) / "with" (for doing something together with someone).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 사과하고 오렌지 있어요? | Do you have apples and oranges? |
| 빵 한 개하고 우유 두 병을 사요. | I buy one loaf of bread and two bottles of milk. |
| 교실에 로라 씨하고 마크 씨가 있어요. | Lora and Mark are in the classroom. |
| 마크 씨하고 같이 갔어요. | I went together with Mark. |

## Unit 6: 어제 일과 — Yesterday's Activities

---

### Pattern: ~았/었-

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 았- (last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ) / 었- (all other vowels) + 어요 → 았어요 / 었어요. 하다 → 했어요.

**Meaning:** Past tense marker — indicates that an action or state occurred in the past.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 어제 뭐 했어요? | What did you do yesterday? |
| 시장에 갔어요. | I went to the market. |
| 사과하고 우유를 샀어요. | I bought apples and milk. |
| 투이 씨는 어제 책을 읽었어요. | Tui read a book yesterday. |
| 치엔 씨는 음악을 들었어요. | Chien listened to music. |

---

### Pattern: 에서

**Form:** Place noun + 에서

**Meaning:** "at / in / from" — marks the location where an action takes place (different from 에 있다 which marks existence).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 어디에서 친구를 만났어요? | Where did you meet your friend? |
| 카페에서 만났어요. | I met them at the café. |
| 로라 씨는 공원에서 운동해요. | Lora exercises in the park. |
| 치엔 씨는 도서관에서 공부했어요. | Chien studied at the library. |

> **Tip:** 에 = existence/destination ("I am at…", "I go to…") vs. 에서 = action location ("I study at…", "I met at…").

## Unit 7: 날씨 — Weather

---

### Pattern: 그리고

**Form:** Sentence 1. 그리고 + Sentence 2.

**Meaning:** "And / In addition" — connects two sentences, adding information. Used at the start of the second sentence.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 여름을 좋아해요. 그리고 봄도 좋아해요. | I like summer. And I also like spring. |
| 저는 학생이에요. 그리고 민수 씨도 학생이에요. | I am a student. And Minsoo is also a student. |
| 저는 한국어를 공부해요. 그리고 중국어도 공부해요. | I study Korean. And I also study Chinese. |

---

### Pattern: 안

**Form:** 안 + Verb / Adjective

**Meaning:** Negation — "not / don't" — placed immediately before the verb or adjective to negate it.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 비가 안 와요. | It's not raining. |
| 오늘은 안 더워요. | It's not hot today. |
| 민수는 오늘 운동을 안 해요. | Minsoo is not exercising today. |
| 학교에 안 가요. | I'm not going to school. |

> **Tip:** For 하다 verbs, 안 goes between the noun and 하다: 운동을 안 해요 (not: 안 운동해요).

## Unit 8: 시간 — Time and Dates

---

### Pattern: 에 (time marker)

**Form:** Time noun + 에

**Meaning:** "at / on / in" — marks the time when an action occurs. Used with specific times, dates, days, and months.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 언제 민수 씨를 만나요? | When are you meeting Minsoo? |
| 오월 오일에 만나요. | I'm meeting him on May 5th. |
| 한국어 수업이 화요일하고 목요일에 있어요. | Korean class is on Tuesday and Thursday. |
| 금요일에 한국에 가요. | I'm going to Korea on Friday. |

> **Note:** 에 is NOT used with relative time words like 오늘 (today), 내일 (tomorrow), 어제 (yesterday), 지금 (now).

---

### Pattern: O시 O분

**Form:** Native Korean number + 시 + Sino-Korean number + 분

**Meaning:** Telling time — 시 = o'clock (hour), 분 = minutes. Native Korean numbers are used for hours; Sino-Korean numbers for minutes.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 지금 몇 시예요? | What time is it now? |
| 다섯 시 오 분이에요. | It is 5:05. |
| 여덟 시 삼십 분에 먹어요. | I eat at 8:30. |
| 세 시 삼십 분에 만나요. | I'm meeting at 3:30. |

> **Tip:** Hours use native Korean numbers (한, 두, 세, 네, 다섯…), minutes use Sino-Korean numbers (일, 이, 삼…).

## Unit 9: 약속 — Making an Appointment

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ까요

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)ㄹ까요

**Meaning:** "Shall we ___? / What do you think about ___?" — used to make suggestions or ask the listener's opinion about a shared activity.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 내일 몇 시에 만날까요? | What time shall we meet tomorrow? |
| 내일 어디에서 만날까요? | Where shall we meet tomorrow? |
| 같이 마크 씨 집에 갈까요? | Shall we go to Mark's place together? |
| 우리 공원에 갈까요? | Shall we go to the park? |

---

### Pattern: ~아/어요 (suggestion form)

**Form:** Verb stem + 아/어요 — same form as the present tense but used as a suggestion when said with a rising or friendly tone.

**Meaning:** "Let's ___" — a gentle suggestion to do something together. Often used in reply to ~(으)ㄹ까요.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 몇 시에 만날까요? 두 시에 만나요. | What time shall we meet? Let's meet at 2. |
| 저녁을 같이 먹어요. 그리고 차도 마셔요. | Let's have dinner together. And let's have tea too. |
| 오늘 저녁 같이 먹을까요? 좋아요. 불고기를 먹어요. | Shall we have dinner? Great. Let's have bulgogi. |

## Unit 10: 주말 활동 — Weekend Activities

---

### Pattern: 그래서

**Form:** Sentence 1. 그래서 + Sentence 2.

**Meaning:** "So / Therefore" — the first sentence is the reason or cause for the second sentence.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 주말에 수영을 배워요. 그래서 수영장에 갔어요. | I'm learning to swim on weekends. So I went to the pool. |
| 저는 한국 영화를 좋아합니다. 그래서 한국어를 공부합니다. | I like Korean movies. So I study Korean. |
| 어제는 많이 아팠어요. 그래서 병원에 갔어요. | I was very sick yesterday. So I went to the hospital. |

> **Tip:** Compare: 그리고 = "and / in addition" (just adds), 그래서 = "so / therefore" (cause → result).

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ 거예요

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)ㄹ 거예요

**Meaning:** "will / going to" — expresses a future plan or intention.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? | What are you going to do this weekend? |
| 친구하고 사진을 찍을 거예요. | I'm going to take photos with a friend. |
| 수진 씨도 미술관에 갈 거예요? | Is Sujin also going to the art gallery? |
| 아니요, 저는 안 갈 거예요. 시간이 없어요. | No, I'm not going to go. I don't have time. |
| 집에서 쉴 거예요. | I'm going to rest at home. |

## Units 11–12: Review Units

> **Unit 11 — 한국어 공부 (Learning Korean):** An integrated speaking and listening unit. Reviews all grammar and vocabulary from Units 1–10 through activities about reasons for studying Korean and personal experiences.

> **Unit 12 — 계획 (Plans):** An integrated speaking, listening, reading, and writing unit. Reviews future tense (~(으)ㄹ 거예요), suggestions (~(으)ㄹ까요), and other patterns from Units 1–10 through activities about making personal plans.

> No new grammar patterns are introduced in Units 11 or 12.
