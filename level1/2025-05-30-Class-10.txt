계절과 날씨 (Seasons & Weather) [gyejeolgwa nalssi]

## Dialogues
Dialogue 1 - Talking about Seasons:
수진: 타완 씨는 봄을 좋아해요? [sujin: tawan ssineun bomeul joahaeyo?]
     → Sujin: Tawan, do you like spring?
타완: 네, 저는 봄을 좋아해요. 수진 씨는요? [tawan: ne, jeoneun bomeul joahaeyo. sujin ssineunyo?]
     → Tawan: Yes, I like spring. Do you?
수진: 저도 봄을 좋아해요. 그리고 가을도 좋아해요. [sujin: jeodo bomeul joahaeyo. geurigo gaeuldo joahaeyo.]
     → Sujin: I like spring too. I also like fall.

Dialogue 2 - Talking about Weather:
민수: 오늘 날씨가 어때요? [minsu: oneul nalssiga eottaeyo?]
     → Minsoo: How is the weather today?
로라: 추워요. [rora: chuwoyo.]
     → Lora: It's cold.
민수: 오늘도 눈이 와요? [minsu: oneuldo nuni wayo?]
     → Minsoo: Is it snowing again today?
로라: 아니요, 눈이 안 와요. [rora: aniyo, nuni an wayo.]
     → Lora: No, it's not snowing.

## Vocabulary

Seasons (계절 - Gyejeol):
- 봄 [bom] → spring (March-May, mild and pleasant)
- 여름 [yeoreum] → summer (June-August, hot and humid with monsoon season)  
- 가을 [gaeul] → fall/autumn (September-November, cool and dry, beautiful autumn colors)
- 겨울 [gyeoul] → winter (December-February, cold with snow, especially in northern regions)

Weather (날씨 - Nalssi):
- 비가 오다 [biga oda] → it rains
  Past: 비가 왔어요 [biga wasseoyo] → it rained
  Present: 비가 와요 [biga wayo] → it rains
  Present (a lot): 비가 많이 와요 [biga mani wayo] → it rains a lot

- 눈이 오다 [nuni oda] → it snows
  Past: 눈이 왔어요 [nuni wasseoyo] → it snowed
  Present: 눈이 와요 [nuni wayo] → it snows

- 날씨가 좋다 [nalssiga jota] → the weather is nice
  Past: 날씨가 좋았어요 [nalssiga joasseoyo] → the weather was nice
  Present: 날씨가 좋아요 [nalssiga joayo] → the weather is nice

- 날씨가 나쁘다 [nalssiga nappeuda] → the weather is bad
  Past: 날씨가 나빴어요 [nalssiga nappasseoyo] → the weather was bad
  Present: 날씨가 나빠요 [nalssiga nappayo] → the weather is bad

- 덥다 [deopda] → it's hot
  Past: 더웠어요 [deowosseoyo] → it was hot
  Present: 더워요 [deowoyo] → it's hot

- 춥다 [chupda] → it's cold
  Past: 추웠어요 [chuwosseoyo] → it was cold
  Present: 추워요 [chuwoyo] → it's cold

- 따뜻하다 [ttatteuthada] → it's warm
  Past: 따뜻했어요 [ttatteuthasseoyo] → it was warm
  Present: 따뜻해요 [ttatteuthaeyo] → it's warm

- 시원하다 [siwonhada] → it's cool
  Past: 시원했어요 [siwonhasseoyo] → it was cool
  Present: 시원해요 [siwonhaeyo] → it's cool

- 바람이 불다 [barami bulda] → it's windy
  Past: 바람이 불었어요 [barami bureosseoyo] → it was windy
  Present: 바람이 불어요 [barami bureoyo] → it's windy

- 흐리다 [heurida] → it's cloudy
  Past: 흐렸어요 [heureosseoyo] → it was cloudy
  Present: 흐려요 [heuryeoyo] → it's cloudy

- 맑다 [makda] → it's clear
  Past: 맑았어요 [malgasseoyo] → it was clear
  Present: 맑아요 [margayo] → it's clear

- 습하다 [seupada] → it's humid
  Past: 습했어요 [seupasseoyo] → it was humid
  Present: 습해요 [seupaeyo] → it's humid

- 폭풍이 치다 [pokpungi chida] → it's stormy
  Past: 폭풍이 쳤어요 [pokpungi chyeosseoyo] → it was stormy
  Present: 폭풍이 쳐요 [pokpungi chyeoyo] → it's stormy

- 안개가 끼다 [angaega kkida] → it's foggy
  Past: 안개가 꼈어요 [angaega kkyeosseoyo] → it was foggy
  Present: 안개가 껴요 [angaega kkyeoyo] → it's foggy

- 무지개가 뜨다 [mujigaega tteuda] → there's a rainbow
  Past: 무지개가 떴어요 [mujigaega tteosseoyo] → there was a rainbow
  Present: 무지개가 떠요 [mujigaega tteoyo] → there's a rainbow

- 우박이 내리다 [ubagi naerida] → it's hailing
  Past: 우박이 내렸어요 [ubagi naeryeosseoyo] → it was hailing
  Present: 우박이 내려요 [ubagi naeryeoyo] → it's hailing

Additional Vocabulary:
- 좋아하다 [joahada] → to like
- 날씨가 어때요? [nalssiga eottaeyo?] → How is the weather?
- 언제 [eonje] → when
- 가져오다 [gajyeooda] → to bring
- 두꺼운 옷 [dukkeoun ot] → thick clothes
- 요즘 [yojeum] → nowadays

## Temperature Reading (온도 읽기 - Ondo Ilgi)

Temperature Vocabulary:
- 온도 [ondo] → temperature
- 도 [do] → degree
- 영상 [yeongsang] → above zero (positive temperature)
- 영하 [yeongha] → below zero (negative temperature)
- 섭씨 [seopsi] → Celsius
- 화씨 [hwasi] → Fahrenheit

Reading Positive Temperatures:
- 20도 [isipdo] → 20 degrees
- 영상 20도 [yeongsang isipdo] → 20 degrees above zero (20°C)
- 영상 5도 [yeongsang odo] → 5 degrees above zero (5°C)

Reading Negative Temperatures:
- 영하 20도 [yeongha isipdo] → 20 degrees below zero (-20°C)
- 영하 5도 [yeongha odo] → 5 degrees below zero (-5°C)
- 영하 1도 [yeongha ildo] → 1 degree below zero (-1°C)

Connection to 지하 (Underground/Basement):
- 지하 [jiha] → underground/basement (literally "below ground")
- 지하철 [jihacheol] → subway (underground train)
- 지하 1층 [jiha ilcheung] → basement level 1 (B1)

Temperature Examples:
- 오늘 온도가 영하 10도예요 [oneul ondoga yeongha sipdo yeyo] → Today's temperature is -10°C
- 어제는 영상 15도였어요 [eojeneun yeongsang sipodo yeosseoyo] → Yesterday it was 15°C
- 겨울에는 영하 20도까지 내려가요 [gyeoure neun yeongha isipdo kkaji naeryeogayo] → In winter it goes down to -20°C

## Grammar Points

1. -도 [do] (also, too)
Attached to a noun to show it is in addition to something else.
Examples:
- 저도 봄을 좋아해요 [jeodo bomeul joahaeyo] → I also like spring
- 가을도 좋아해요 [gaeuldo joahaeyo] → I also like fall
- 민수 씨도 학생이에요 [minsu ssido haksaengieyo] → Minsoo is also a student

2. 그리고 [geurigo] (and, and then)
Used to connect two sentences or clauses.
Examples:
- 저는 학생이에요. 그리고 선생님이에요. [jeoneun haksaengieyo. geurigo seonsaengnimieyo.]
  → I am a student. And I am a teacher.
- 저는 학생이에요. 그리고 민수 씨도 학생이에요. [jeoneun haksaengieyo. geurigo minsu ssido haksaengieyo.]
  → I'm a student. And Minsoo is also a student.
- 저는 한국어를 공부해요. 그리고 중국어도 공부해요. [jeoneun hangugeoreul gongbuhaeyo. geurigo junggugeoedo gongbuhaeyo.]
  → I study Korean. And I also study Chinese.

Difference between 그리고 [geurigo] and 하고 [hago]:
- 그리고 connects sentences/clauses
- 하고 connects nouns only
Example with 하고:
- 저는 사과하고 바나나를 좋아해요. [jeoneun sagwahago bananareul joahaeyo.]
  → I like apples and bananas.

3. 안 [an] (negation)
안 is used for "will not" or "do not" to express a negative action by choice or volition.

Examples with 안:
- 피자를 안 먹어요 [pijareul an meogeoyo] → I won't eat pizza (by choice)
- 눈이 안 와요 [nuni an wayo] → It is not snowing
- 오늘은 학교에 안 가요 [oneureun hakgyoe an gayo] → I'm not going to school today (by choice)
- 커피를 안 마셔요 [keopireul an masyeoyo] → I don't drink coffee (personal preference)
- 텔레비전을 안 봐요 [tellebijoneul an bwayo] → I don't watch television (decision)

Difference with 못 [mot]:
While 안 is used for voluntary negation, 못 is used for "cannot" due to inability or circumstances beyond one's control.

Example comparing 안 and 못:
- 나는 술을 안 마셔요 [naneun sureul an masyeoyo] → I don't drink alcohol (by choice)
- 나는 술을 못 마셔요 [naneun sureul mot masyeoyo] → I cannot drink alcohol (due to medical condition or allergy)

## Practice Examples & Useful Phrases

Season Preferences:
- 봄을 좋아해요? [bomeul joahaeyo?] → Do you like spring?
- 네, 봄을 좋아해요. 그리고 여름도 좋아해요. [ne, bomeul joahaeyo. geurigo yeoreumdo joahaeyo.]
  → Yes, I like spring. And I also like summer.

Weather Questions & Answers:
- 오늘 날씨가 어때요? [oneul nalssiga eottaeyo?] → How is the weather today?
- 요즘 날씨가 어때요? [yojeum nalssiga eottaeyo?] → How is the weather these days?
- 오늘도 눈이 와요? [oneuldo nuni wayo?] → Is it snowing again today?
  → 아니요, 눈이 안 와요. [aniyo, nuni an wayo.] → No, it's not snowing.
- 오늘도 날씨가 좋아요? [oneuldo nalssiga joahaeyo?] → Is the weather nice again today?
  → 아니요, 날씨가 나빠요. 비가 와요. [aniyo, nalssiga nappayo. biga wayo.] → No, the weather is bad. It's raining.

Seasonal Descriptions:
- 봄은 따뜻해요 [bomeun ttatteuthaeyo] → Spring is warm
- 여름은 더워요 [yeoreumeun deowoyo] → Summer is hot
- 가을은 시원해요 [gaeureun siwonhaeyo] → Fall is cool
- 겨울은 추워요 [gyeoureun chuwoyo] → Winter is cold

Travel & Clothing:
- 언제 한국에 와요? [eonje hanguge wayo?] → When are you coming to Korea?
- 두꺼운 옷을 가져오세요 [dukkeoun oseul gajyeooseyo] → Please bring thick clothes
- 한국 겨울 날씨는 추워요 [hanguk gyeoul nalssineun chuwoyo] → Korean winter weather is cold
