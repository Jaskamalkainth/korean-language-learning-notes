- Three level of formality for each VERB
Dictionary Form (base verb)
Casual/Polite (요-ending, 존댓말)
Formal 습니다 / 입니다-ending, highest politeness

1. to go, 가다 (gada), 가요 (gayo), 갑니다 (gamnida)
2. to meet, 만나다 (mannada), 만나요 (mannayo), 만납니다 (mannamnida)
3. to read, 읽다 (ilgda), 읽어요 (ilgeoyo), 읽습니다 (ilgseumnida)
4. to come, 오다 (oda), 와요 (wayo), 옵니다 (omnida)
5. to write, 쓰다 (sseuda), 써요 (sseoyo), 씁니다 (sseumnida)
6. to sleep, 자다 (jada), 자요 (jayo), 잡니다 (jamnida)
7. to see/watch, 보다 (boda), 봐요 (bwayo), 봅니다 (bomnida)
8. to eat, 먹다 (meokda), 먹어요 (meogeoyo), 먹습니다 (meokseumnida)
9. to drink, 마시다 (masida), 마셔요 (masyeoyo), 마십니다 (masimnida)
10. to drive, 운전하다 (unjeonhada), 운전해요 (unjeonhaeyo), 운전합니다 (unjeonhamnida)
11. to contact, 연락하다 (yeollakada), 연락해요 (yeollakhaeyo), 연락합니다 (yeollakhamnida)

Korean Greetings and Expressions

감사합니다 — kamsahamnida — Thank you (formal)
고맙습니다 — gomapseumnida — Thank you (formal polite)
고마워요 — gomawoyo — Thank you (informal polite)
고마워 — gomawo — Thank you (casual)

바쁘세요? — bappeuseyo? — Are you busy? (honorific)

처음 뵙겠습니다 — cheoeum boepgetseumnida — Nice to meet you (humble, first meeting)
반갑습니다 — bangapseumnida — Nice to meet you (formal)

안녕 — annyeong — Hello (casual)
안녕하세요 — annyeonghaseyo — Hello/Goodbye (formal)
식사하셨어요? — siksahasyeosseoyo? — Have you eaten? (honorific form of "Did you eat?")
밥 먹었어요? — bap meogeosseoyo? — Have you eaten? (common greeting)
요즘 어떻게 지내세요? — yojeum eotteoke jinaeseyo? — How are you doing these days?

안녕히 가세요 — annyeonghi gaseyo — Go well/Goodbye (when listener leaves)
안녕히 계세요 — annyeonghi gyeseyo — Stay well/Goodbye (when speaker leaves)
다음에 또 뵙겠습니다 — daeume tto boepgetseumnida — See you next time
다시 봅시다 — dasi bopseuda — See you again
다음에 연락할게요 — daeum-e yeonlakhalgeyo — Will contact you again
조심해서 들어가세요 — josimhaeseo deurogaseyo — Take care going in/Enter carefully
먼저 일어나겠습니다 — meonjeo ireonagetsseumnida — I am leaving first

무소식이 희소식이다 — musosigi huisosigida — No news is good news (proverb)

Key Cultural Notes:
Bowing degrees: 15° (light), 30° (standard), 45° (sincere)
"Bellybutton greeting": Placing hands over bellybutton while bowing for added sincerity
Speech levels vary based on formality and relationship between speakers
Younger/junior person should initiate greetings
Confucian (Chinese philosopher Confucius) influence emphasizes respect in relationships (2500+ years)
Honorific and humble speech forms express varying levels of respect
Common greeting "Have you eaten?" shows cultural concern for others' wellbeing
