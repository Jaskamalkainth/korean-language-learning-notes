## Dialogues

Dialogue 1 - Past Weekend Activities:
지연: 수진 씨, 주말에 뭐 했어요? [jiyeon: sujin ssi, jumare mwo haesseoyo?]
     → Jiyeon: Sujin, what did you do last weekend?
수진: 고향에서 친구가 왔어요. 그래서 같이 박물관에 갔어요. [sujin: gohyangeseo chinguga wasseoyo. geuraeseo gachi bangmulgwane gasseoyo.]
     → Sujin: My friend visited me from her hometown. So I went to a museum with her.
지연: 지엔 씨는 뭐 했어요? [jiyeon: jien ssineun mwo haesseoyo?]
     → Jiyeon: What did you do, Chien?
지엔: 저는 집에서 책을 읽었어요. [jien: jeoneun jibeseo chaekeul ilgeosseoyo.]
     → Chien: I stayed at home and read a book.

Dialogue 2 - Future Weekend Plans:
마크: 민수 씨, 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? [makeu: minsu ssi, jumare mwo hal geoyeyo?]
     → Mark: Minsoo, what will you do this weekend?
민수: 축구를 할 거예요. 마크 씨는 뭐 할 거예요? [minsu: chukgureul hal geoyeyo. makeu ssineun mwo hal geoyeyo?]
     → Minsoo: I will play soccer. What about you, Mark?
마크: 저는 약속이 없어요. 그래서 집에서 쉴 거예요. [makeu: jeoneun yaksogi eopseoyo. geuraeseo jibeseo swil geoyeyo.]
     → Mark: I don't have any plans. So I will rest at home.
민수: 그러면 우리랑 같이 축구를 해요. [minsu: geureomyeon urirang gachi chukgureul haeyo.]
     → Minsoo: Then, why don't you play soccer with us?

## Grammar Points

**1. 그래서 [geuraeseo]**
Usage: Used between sentences to show cause and effect (So/Therefore)
- First sentence = reason, second sentence = result
Examples:
- 저는 영화를 좋아해요. 그래서 한국 영화를 공부합니다. [jeoneun yeonghwareul joahaeyo. geuraeseo hanguk yeonghwareul gongbuhabnida.] → I like movies. So I study Korean movies.
- 많이 아팠어요. 그래서 병원에 갔어요. [mani apasseoyo. geuraeseo byeongwone gasseoyo.] → I was very sick. So I went to the hospital.
- 고향에서 친구가 왔어요. 그래서 같이 박물관에 갔어요. [gohyangeseo chinguga wasseoyo. geuraeseo gachi bangmulgwane gasseoyo.] → My friend came from hometown. So we went to the museum together.

**2. (~을) 거예요 [-(eul) geoyeyo]**
Usage: Attached to verb root to indicate FUTURE plans or predictions
Formation:
- Verb stem ending in consonant + 을 거예요
- Verb stem ending in vowel + ㄹ 거예요
Examples:
- 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? [jumare mwo hal geoyeyo?] → What will you do on the weekend?
- 친구하고 사진을 찍을 거예요. [chinguhago sajineul jjigeul geoyeyo.] → I will take pictures with my friend.
- 저는 안 갈 거예요. [jeoneun an gal geoyeyo.] → I won't go.
- 집에서 쉴 거예요. [jibeseo swil geoyeyo.] → I will rest at home.

**3. -겠어요 [-gesseoyo] - Simple Future**
Usage: Simpler way to express future intention or supposition (I will...)
- Easier to use than ~(을) 거예요
- Shows speaker's intention or assumption about the future
Formation:
- Verb stem + 겠어요
Examples:
- 내일 가겠어요. [naeil gagesseoyo.] → I will go tomorrow.
- 주말에 읽겠어요. [jumare ilgesseoyo.] → I will read on the weekend.
- 사진을 찍겠어요. [sajineul jjigesseoyo.] → I will take pictures.

## Useful Phrases

**Asking About Weekend Activities:**
- 주말에 뭐 했어요? [jumare mwo haesseoyo?] → What did you do on the weekend?
- 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? [jumare mwo hal geoyeyo?] → What will you do on the weekend?
- 지난 주말에 뭐 했어요? [jinan jumare mwo haesseoyo?] → What did you do last weekend?
- 이번 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? [ibeun jumare mwo hal geoyeyo?] → What will you do this weekend?

**Describing Activities:**
- 축구를 할 거예요 [chukgureul hal geoyeyo] → I will play soccer
- 사진을 찍을 거예요 [sajineul jjigeul geoyeyo] → I will take pictures
- 집에서 책을 읽었어요 [jibeseo chaekeul ilgeosseoyo] → I read a book at home
- 박물관에 갔어요 [bangmulgwane gasseoyo] → I went to the museum

**Talking About Plans:**
- 약속이 있어요? [yaksogi isseoyo?] → Do you have plans?
- 네, 있어요 [ne, isseoyo] → Yes, I do
- 아니요, 없어요 [aniyo, eopseoyo] → No, I don't
- 시간이 있어요 [sigani isseoyo] → I have time
- 시간이 없어요 [sigani eopseoyo] → I don't have time

**Making Invitations:**
- 우리랑 같이 해요 [urirang gachi haeyo] → Do it with us
- 같이 축구를 해요 [gachi chukgureul haeyo] → Play soccer with us
- 같이 가요 [gachi gayo] → Let's go together
- 같이 밥도 먹어요 [gachi bapdo meogeoyo] → Let's eat together too

**Expressing Reasons:**
- 왜 [place]에 갔어요? [wae [place]e gasseoyo?] → Why did you go to [place]?
- [reason]. 그래서 [result]. [[reason]. geuraeseo [result].] → [reason]. So [result].

**Sample Sentences:**
- 이 책을 읽었어요? [i chaekeul ilgeosseoyo?] → Have you read this book?
- 주말에 읽을 거예요. [jumare ilgeul geoyeyo.] → I will read over the weekend.
- 일이 있어요. 그래서 사무실에 갈 거예요. [iri isseoyo. geuraeseo samusire gal geoyeyo.] → I have work. Therefore I will go to the office.

**Additional Notes:**
- Past tense: 했어요 [haesseoyo] → did
- Future tense: 할 거예요 [hal geoyeyo] → will do / -겠어요 [-gesseoyo] → will do (simpler)
- Use 그래서 to connect cause and effect naturally in conversation
