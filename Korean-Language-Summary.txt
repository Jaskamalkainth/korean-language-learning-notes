KOREAN LANGUAGE COMPREHENSIVE SUMMARY
====================================

HANGUL CHARACTERS
-------------------

BASIC VOWELS (10):
ㅏ (a), ㅑ (ya), ㅓ (eo), ㅕ (yeo), ㅗ (o), ㅛ (yo), ㅜ (u), ㅠ (yu), ㅡ (eu), ㅣ (i)

BASIC CONSONANTS (14):
ㄱ (g/k), ㄴ (n), ㄷ (d/t), ㄹ (r/l), ㅁ (m), ㅂ (b/p), ㅅ (s), ㅇ (silent/ng), ㅈ (j), ㅊ (ch), ㅋ (k), ㅌ (t), ㅍ (p), ㅎ (h)

COMPLEX CONSONANTS (5):
ㄲ (kk) – Stronger than ㄱ
ㄸ (tt) – Stronger than ㄷ
ㅃ (pp) – Stronger than ㅂ
ㅆ (ss) – Stronger than ㅅ
ㅉ (jj) – Stronger than ㅈ

COMPLEX VOWELS (11):
ㅐ (ae) – Like "a" in "care"
ㅒ (yae) – Like "ya" in "yam"
ㅔ (e) – Like "e" in "bed"
ㅖ (ye) – Like "ye" in "yes"
ㅘ (wa) – Like "wa" in "water"
ㅙ (wae) – Like "we" in "wet"
ㅚ (oe) – Like "we" in "wet" (similar to ㅙ)
ㅝ (wo) – Like "wo" in "wonder"
ㅞ (we) – Like "we" in "wedding"
ㅟ (wi) – Like "wee" in "week"
ㅢ (ui) – Like "ui" in "gooey"

IMPORTANT RULES
-----------------

BATCHIM RULES (7 Final Sounds):
1. ㄱ,ㅋ,ㄲ → [k]: 수박 (su-bak) = watermelon
2. ㄴ → [n]: 안 (an) = inside
3. ㄷ,ㅅ,ㅆ,ㅈ,ㅊ,ㅌ,ㅎ → [t]: 옷 (ot) = clothes
4. ㄹ → [l]: 달 (dal) = moon
5. ㅁ → [m]: 밤 (bam) = night/chestnut
6. ㅂ,ㅍ → [p]: 집 (jip) = house
7. ㅇ → [ng]: 공 (gong) = ball

CONSONANT ASSIMILATION RULES:
1. ㅂ + ㄴ → [m-n]: 입니다 = [임니다] (im-ni-da)
2. 같이 → [가치] (ga-chi) = together

ㅎ NEUTRALIZATION RULE:
- Final Position: ㅎ becomes silent (좋다 → [조타])
- Before Consonants: ㅎ is typically silent (않고 → [안꼬])

DOUBLE BATCHIM RULES:
- When at word end or before consonant: usually only one consonant pronounced
- When followed by vowel: second consonant moves to next syllable

GRAMMAR MARKERS
-----------------
이/가 (Subject Marker)
- Consonant + 이
- Vowel + 가

은/는 (Topic Marker)
- Consonant + 은
- Vowel + 는

을/를 (Object Marker)
- Consonant + 을
- Vowel + 를

에 (Location/Time Marker)
- Used to indicate location or time

KOREAN PROVERBS
----------------
- 꿩 대신 닭 (Kkwong daesin dak) - "Chicken instead of pheasant"
  Meaning: When you can't get what you want, you should be satisfied with the next best thing.
  Similar to English: "A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush" or "Better than nothing"

- 옷이 날개다 (Osi nalgaeda) - "Clothes are wings"
  Meaning: Clothes give you wings - suggesting that proper attire can help you achieve your goals

- 그림의 떡 (Geulim-ui tteok) - "Rice cake in a picture"
  Meaning: Similar to "pie in the sky" - something that looks good but is unattainable

- 무소식이 희소식이다 (Musosigi huisosigida) - "No news is good news"
  Meaning: When you don't hear anything, it usually means everything is fine

- 운는 집에 보기 있다 (Unneun jibe bogi itda) - "A happy home begets good luck"
  Meaning: A harmonious and happy home brings good fortune
