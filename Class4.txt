KOREAN HOMES VOCABULARY:

Traditional Housing:
한옥 [hanok] -> Traditional Korean house
마루 [maru] -> Raised hardwood floor area for air circulation
온돌 [ondol] -> Traditional underfloor heating system
안채 [anche] -> Women's quarters in traditional homes
사랑채 [sarangche] -> Men's quarters in traditional homes
한지 [hanji] -> Traditional Korean paper used for doors and windows

Modern Housing:
아파트 [apateu] -> Apartment (high-rise)
빌라 [bila] -> Small apartment building (4 floors or fewer)

원룸 아파트 [wonnum pata] -> Studio/one-room apartment
복층 아파트 [bokcheung-apata] -> Split-level(loft, multi story) apartment
오피스텔 [opiseu-tel] -> Multipurpose living/work units

Student Housing:
고시원 [goshiwon] -> Budget student accommodation with small rooms
하숙집 [hasukjib] -> Boarding house with meals provided

Measurements and Concepts:
평 [pyeong] -> Traditional Korean unit of floor measurement (3.3 sq meters)

Common Expressions:
살다 [salda] -> To live (in a place)
어디에서 살아요? [eodie-seo sarayo?] -> Where do you live?
서울에서 살아요 [seuoreseo sarayo] -> I live in Seoul

이사하다 [isahada] -> To move (houses)
홍대에서 신촌으로 이사해요 [hongdaeeseo sinchoneuro isahaeyo] -> I am moving from Hongdae to Sinchon

Proverbs About Home:
운는 집에 보기 있다 [Unneun jibe bogi itda] -> A happy home begets good luck
