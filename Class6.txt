위치 (Locations and Positions)

Dialogue 1:
책상이 있어요? [chaeksangi isseoyo?] -> Is there a desk?
네, 책상이 있어요. [ne, chaeksangi isseoyo.] -> Yes, there is a desk.
의자가 있어요? [uijaga isseoyo?] -> Is there a chair?
네, 의자가 있어요. [ne, uijaga isseoyo.] -> Yes, there is a chair.

Dialogue 2:
책이 어디에 있어요? [chaegi eodie isseoyo?] -> Where is the book?
책이 교실에 있어요. [chaegi gyosire isseoyo.] -> The book is in the classroom.
책상 위에 있어요? [chaeksang wie isseoyo?] -> Is it on the desk?
아니요. 책상 위에 없어요. 가방 안에 있어요. [aniyo. chaeksang wie eopseoyo. gabang ane isseoyo.] -> No, it's not on the desk. It's in the bag.

GRAMMAR:
이/가 (Subject Marker)
- Consonant + 이
- Vowel + 가
examples:
책이 [chaeki] (book)
의자가 [uijaga] (chair)
선생님이 [seonsaengnimi] (teacher)
가방이 [gabangi] (bag)
학교가 [hakgyoga] (school)


에 있다/없다 (Location/Existence)
examples:
네, 있어요. [ne, isseoyo.] -> Yes, I have it.
아니요, 없어요. [aniyo, eopseoyo.] -> No, I don't have it.

Extra:
저는 학생입니다. [Jeoneun haksaeng imnida] — I am a student.

## Korean "Happy Birthday" song
생일 축하합니다 [Saengil chukahamnida] — Happy birthday to you
사랑하는 (name) 씨 [Saranghaneun (name) ssi] — Dear (name)

Vocab:
생일 (saengil) = birthday
축하하다 (chukahada) = to congratulate
사랑하다 (saranghada) = to love
사랑하는 (saranghaneun) = beloved/dear

- Common birthday greetings:
  - 생일 축하해요 (Saengil chukahaeyo) - informal "Happy Birthday"
  - 생일 축하합니다 (Saengil chukahamnida) - formal "Happy Birthday"

## "Three Bears Song" (곰 세 마리)
곰 세 마리가 한 집에 있어 [Gom se mari-ga han jib-e isseo] — Three bears are in one house
아빠 곰 [Appa gom] — Daddy bear
엄마 곰 [Eomma gom] — Mommy bear
애기 곰 [Aegi gom] — Baby bear
아빠 곰은 뚱뚱해 [Appa gom-eun ttungttungha-e] — Daddy bear is fat
엄마 곰은 날씬해 [Eomma gom-eun nalssinha-e] — Mommy bear is slim
애기 곰은 너무 귀여워 [Aegi gom-eun neomu gwiyeowo] — Baby bear is very cute
으쓱 으쓱 잘한다 [Eusseuk eusseuk jalhanda] — Wiggle wiggle well done!

Vocab:
곰 (gom) = bear
세 마리 (se mari) = three animals (animal counter 마리)
한 집 (han jib) = one house
뚱뚱하다 (ttungttunghada) = to be fat
날씬하다 (nalssinhada) = to be slim
귀엽다 (gwiyeopda) = to be cute
으쓱으쓱 (eusseuk eusseuk) = wiggle/move shoulders proudly

KOREAN HOLIDAYS (명절 - MYEONGJEOL):

1. 추석 (Chuseok) - Korean Harvest Festival
   - Also known as 한가위 (Hangawi)
   - Falls on 15th day of 8th lunar month (Sept/Oct)
   - Three-day celebration of autumn harvest
   - Traditions: 차례 (Charye) - ancestor memorial service, 벌초 (Beolcho/Polco) - visiting/cleaning family graves
   - Special table prepared with fruits, meat, vegetables, rice and rice cakes for ancestors
   - Foods: 송편 (Songpyeon) - half-moon rice cakes filled with sesame seeds and black beans, steamed over pine needles
          전 (Jeon) - savory pancakes
          배 (Bae) - Korean pears
          감 (Gam) - persimmons
   - Gift-giving: beautifully packaged boxes of fruits and meats
   - Heavy travel to 고향 (kohang) - hometown

2. 설날 (Seollal) - Lunar New Year
   - Celebrated in January/February according to lunar calendar
   - Three-day celebration
   - Traditions: 차례 (Charye) - ancestor memorial service
              한복 (Hanbok) - wearing traditional Korean clothing
              세배 (Sebae) - deep bow to elders
   - New Year's greeting: 새해 복 많이 받으세요 (Saehae bok mani badeuseyo) - "May you receive many blessings in the new year"
   - 세뱃돈 (Sebaetdon) - money given to children after they perform sebae (New Year's bow)
   - 복주머니 (Bokjumeoni) - traditional silk pouches used to give money to children
   - 덕담 (Deokdam) - words of blessing and good wishes exchanged during Seollal
   - Greeting: 복 많이 받으세요 (Bok mani badeuseyo) - "Receive many blessings"
   - Children receive money in silk bags called 복주머니 (Bokjumeoni)
   - Foods: 떡국 (Tteokguk) - rice cake soup (symbolizes gaining a year in age)
          갈비탕 (Galbitang) - beef short rib soup
          잡채 (Japchae) - stir-fried glass noodles
   - 한과 (Hangwa) - traditional Korean confectionery
   - Traditional games: 윷놀이 (Yunnori) - board game
                      제기차기 (Jegi Chagi/Tegi Tagi) - traditional kicking game

3. National Holidays:
   - March 1: 독립 운동일 (Dongnip Undongil) - Independence Movement Day
   - July 17: 제헌절 (Jeheonjeol) - Constitution Day
   - August 15: 광복절 (Gwangbokjeol) - National Liberation Day
   - October 3: 개천절 (Gaecheonjeol) - National Foundation Day
   - October 9: 한글날 (Hangeulnal) - Hangul Day (celebrating King Sejong's creation of Korean alphabet in 1443)

4. Other Significant Days:
   - April 5: 식목일 (Singmogil) - Arbor Day
   - May 5: 어린이날 (Eorininal) - Children's Day (special events and free admission for children)
   - May 8: 어버이날 (Eobeoinal) - Parents Day
   - June 6: 현충일 (Hyeonchungil) - Memorial Day (honoring those who served)
   - October 1: 국군의 날 (Gukgunui Nal) - Armed Forces Day
   - 정월 대보름 (Jeongwol Daeboreum) - first full moon of the lunar year

COMMON PHRASES:
- 설날/추석 잘 보내세요 (Seollal/Chuseok jal bonaeseyo) - Have a good Seollal/Chuseok
- 고향이 어디예요? (Kohyang-i eodiyeyo?) - Where is your hometown?
- 고향에 자주 가요? (Kohyang-e jaju gayo?) - Do you go to your hometown often?

KOREAN PROVERBS:
- 꿩 대신 닭 (Kkwong daesin dak) - "Chicken instead of pheasant"
  Meaning: When you can't get what you want, you should be satisfied with the next best thing.
  Similar to English: "A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush" or "Better than nothing"
