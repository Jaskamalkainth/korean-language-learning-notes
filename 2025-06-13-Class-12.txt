약속 (Appointment) [yaksok]
제안하기 (Making a suggestion) [jeanhagi]

## Dialogues

Dialogue 1 - Making an Appointment:
민수: 내일 몇 시에 만날까요? [minsu: naeil myeot sie mannalkkayo?]
     → Minsu: What time do you want to meet tomorrow?
투이: 한 시 어때요? [tui: han sie eottaeyo?]
     → Tui: How about 1 p.m.?
민수: 미안해요. 오전에는 일이 있어요. 오후는 어때요? [minsu: mianhaeyo. ojeoneneun iri isseoyo. ohuneun eottaeyo?]
     → Minsu: I'm sorry, I have something to do in the morning. How about the afternoon?
투이: 좋아요. 두 시에 만날까요? [tui: joayo. du sie mannalkkayo?]
     → Tui: Okay. Can we meet at 2 p.m.?
민수: 네, 좋아요. [minsu: ne, joayo.]
     → Minsu: Sure.

Dialogue 2 - Making Plans:
민수: 오늘 오후에 뭐 할까요? [minsu: oneul ohue mwo halkkayo?]
     → Minsu: What do you want to do this afternoon?
수진: 네, 좋아요. 뭐 먹을까요? [sujin: ne, joayo. mwo meogeulkkayo?]
     → Sujin: Sure. What should we eat?
민수: 삼겹살 어때요? [minsu: samgyeopsal eottaeyo?]
     → Minsu: How about samgyeopsal?
수진: 좋아요. 몇 시에 만날까요? [sujin: joayo. myeot sie mannalkkayo?]
     → Sujin: Sounds good. What time shall we meet?
민수: 다섯 시에 어때요? [minsu: daseot sie eottaeyo?]
     → Minsu: How about 5 o'clock?
수진: 네, 좋아요. 5층에서 봐요. [sujin: ne, joayo. ocheungeseo bwayo.]
     → Sujin: Okay, let's meet on the 5th floor.

## Vocabulary

**A. Appointment Related (약속 관련)**
- 약속이 있다 [yaksogi itda] → to have an appointment
- 약속이 없다 [yaksogi eopda] → not to have an appointment
- 시간이 있다 [sigani itda] → to have time
- 시간이 없다 [sigani eopda] → to not have time
- 일이 있다 [iri itda] → to have work
- 바쁘다 [bappeuda] → to be busy
- 괜찮다 [gwaenchanta] → to be okay

**B. Activities (활동)**
- 점심을 먹다 [jeomsimeul meokda] → to have lunch
- 영화를 보다 [yeonghwareul boda] → to watch a movie
- 쇼핑을 하다 [syopingeul hada] → to go shopping

## Grammar Points

**1. -(으)ㄹ까요? [-(eu)lkkayo?]**
Usage: Making suggestions or asking for someone's opinion
Examples:
- 같이 점심을 먹을까요? [gachi jeomsimeul meogeulkkayo?] → Shall we have lunch together?
- 오후에 만날까요? [ohue mannalkkayo?] → Shall we meet in the afternoon?

**2. -아요/어요 [-ayo/eoyo]**
Usage: Polite ending for present tense verbs
Examples:
- 만나요 [mannayo] → to meet
- 먹어요 [meogeoyo] → to eat
- 봐요 [bwayo] → to see/watch

**3. -자 [-ja] / -세다 [-seuda]**
Usage: Used to express "let's" for making suggestions
Examples:
- 주말에 나가자 [jumare nagaja] / 주말에 나가세다 [jumare nagaseda] → Let's go out on the weekend
- 주말에 일하자 [jumare ilhaja] / 주말에 일하세다 [jumare ilhaseda] → Let's work over the weekend

**4. -고 싶어요 [-go sipeoyo]**
Usage: Expressing desire or want
Examples:
- 삼성 회사에서 일하고 싶어요 [samseong hoesaseo ilhago sipeoyo] → I want to work for Samsung company

**5. 되다 [doeda]**
Usage: To become
Examples:
- 의사가 되고 싶어요 [uisaga doego sipeoyo] → I want to become a doctor
- 가수가 되고 싶어요 [gasuga doego sipeoyo] → I want to become a singer
- 철학자가 되고 싶어요 [cheolhakjaga doego sipeoyo] → I want to become a philosopher

## Useful Phrases

**Schedule Related:**
- 일이 있어요 [iri isseoyo] → I have work
- 바빠요 [bappayo] → I am busy
- 지금 시간이 있어요 [jigeum sigani isseoyo] → I have time now
- 일곱 시에 어때요? [ilgop sie eottaeyo?] → How about 7 o'clock?
- 일요일에 시간 있어요? [ireoril shigen isseyo?] → What time on Sunday?

**Activity Suggestions:**
- 영화를 볼까요? [yeonghwareul bolkkayo?] → Shall we watch a movie?
- 집에 갈까요? [jibe galkkayo?] → Shall we go home?
- 주말에 쇼핑 갈까요? [jumare syoping galkkayo?] → Shall we go shopping on the weekend?
- 같이 읽을까요? [gachi ilgeulkkayo?] → Shall we read together?

**Making Suggestions:**
- 어때요? [eottaeyo?] → How about it?
- 같이 할까요? [gachi halkkayo?] → Shall we do it together?
- 몇 시에 만날까요? [myeot sie mannalkkayo?] → What time shall we meet?

**Responding to Suggestions:**
- 좋아요 [joayo] → Sounds good
- 네, 좋아요 [ne, joayo] → Yes, that's good
- 미안해요 [mianhaeyo] → I'm sorry
- 좋아요 [johayeo] → Sounds great
- 괜찮아요 [gwaenchhayo] → That's ok

**Note:**
- 아침 [achim] → morning
- 먹을 [mogeul] → meal
- 먹다 [mokda] → to eat (used for breakfast as 아침을 먹다 [achimeul mokda] → to have breakfast)
