CHAPTER - 안부 [anbu] (Saying Hello) - Continued!

## TLDR - Key Points Covered:
• **Connecting Sentences**: Using 그런데 [geureonde] (but/however) to link contrasting ideas
• **Korean Counters**: Units for people (명/분), objects (개), animals (마리), books (권)
• **Text/Email Practice**: 보내는 사람 [bonaeneun saram] (sender), 받는 사람 [batneun saram] (receiver)
• **Grammar Patterns**:
  - **에게/에게서** [ege/egeseo] (to/from someone)
  - **때문에** [ttaemune] (because of) + noun
  - **수 있어요/없어요** [su isseoyo/eopseoyo] (can/cannot)
  - **안 vs 못** [an vs mot] (choice vs inability)
  - **(으)ㄹ까요** [(eu)lkkayo] (shall we/shall I)

### 📚 Homework - Page 17: Connecting Sentences
관련된 문장들 [gwallyeondoen munjangdeul] (Connecting sentences)

**1.** 이번 주에는 회사 일이 많아요. 많이 바빠요.
→ 그런데 다음 주에는 시간이 좀 있어요. 즐거워요.

Ibeon ju-eneun hoesa iri manayo. mani bappayo. geureonde daeum ju-eneun sigani jom isseoyo. jeulgeowoyo.
This week, I have a lot of work, I'm very busy. But next week, I have some time. I'm happy.

**2.** 요즘 주말에 운동을 해요.
→ 그런데 이번 주말에는 운동을 안 해요. 주말에 친구와 여행을 갈 거예요.

Yojeum jumare undongeul haeyo. geureonde ibeon jumare-neun undongeul an haeyo. jumare chinguwa yeohaengeul gal geoyeyo.
These days, I exercise on weekends. But this weekend, I won't exercise. I'll go on a trip with my friend.

**3.** 친구들은 오늘 영화관에 가요.
→ 그런데 저는 많이 피곤해요. 집에서 쉴 거예요.

Chingudeureun oneul yeonghwagwane gayo. geureonde jeoneun mani pigonhaeyo. jibeseo swil geoyeyo.
My friends are going to the cinema today. But I am very tired. I'll rest at home.

**4.** 한국어 말하기는 재미있어요.
→ 그런데 한국어 듣기는 조금 어려워요.

Hangugeo malhagineun jaemiisseoyo. geureonde hangugeo deutgineun jogeum eoryeowoyo.
Speaking Korean is fun. But listening to Korean is a little difficult.

**Dialogue:**
가: 요즘 어떻게 지내요? [ga: yojeum eotteoke jinaeyo?]
     → How are you these days?
나: 이번 주에는 좀 바빠어요. 그런데 다음 주는 휴가예요. 여행을 갈 거예요. [na: iban ju-eneun jom bappasseoyo. geureonde daeum ju-neun hyugayeyo. yeohaengeul gal geoyeyo.]
     → This week I was a little busy. But next week is holiday. I will go on a trip.

📱 문자 메시지 (Text message)
민수 씨, 미안해요. 전화를 못 받았어요. 제가 요즘 많이 바빴어요. 회사에 일이 많아요. 내일 다시 전화해요.
Minsu-ssi, mianhaeyo. Jeonhwareul mot badasseoyo. Jega yojeum manhi bappasseoyo. Hosa-e iri manayo. Naeil dasi jeonhwahaeyo.
→ Mr. Minsu, sorry. I couldn't take the call. I am busy these days. There is a lot of work at the company. I will call again tomorrow.

Q1. 누가 누구에게 문자 메시지를 보냈어요?
Nuga nuguege munja mesijireul bonaesseoyo?
→ Who sent the message to whom?
👉 투이가 민수에게 보냈어요.
Tuiga Minsu-ege bonaesseoyo.
→ Tui sent it to Minsu.

Q2. 투이 씨는 요즘 어떻게 지내요?
Tui-ssi-neun yojeum eotteoke jinaeyo?
→ How is Tui these days?
👉 일이 많아서 바빠요.
Iri manaseo bappayo.
→ Busy because of a lot of work.

📧 이메일 (Email)
수진 씨, 안녕하세요? 그동안 잘 지냈어요? 저는 요즘 한국어 공부도 하고 운동도 해요. 한국어가 재미있어요. 그래서 즐거워요. 그런데 수진 씨는 언제 일본에 올 거예요? 일본 음식을 같이 먹고 여행도 같이 가요. 그럼 안녕히 계세요. – 유키
Sujin-ssi, annyeonghaseyo? Geudongan jal jinaesseoyo? Jeoneun yojeum hangugeo gongbudo hago undongdo haeyo. Hangugeo-ga jaemi isseoyo. Geuraeseo jeulgeowoyo. Geureonde Sujin-ssi-neun eonje Ilbone ol geoyeyo? Ilbon eumsigeul gachi meokgo yeohaengdo gachi gayo. Geureom annyeonghi gyeseyo. – Yuki
→ Hello Sujin, how are you? These days I study Korean and exercise. Korean is fun. So I'm happy. When will you come to Japan? Let's eat Japanese food and travel together. Goodbye. – Yuki

Q1. 유키 씨는 요즘 어떤 일을 해요?
Yuki-ssi-neun yojeum eotteon ireul haeyo?
→ What does Yuki do these days?
👉 한국어 공부도 하고 운동도 해요.
Hangugeo gongbudo hago undongdo haeyo.
→ Studying Korean and exercising.

Q2. 수진 씨는 지금 일본에 있어요?
Sujin-ssi-neun jigeum Ilbone isseoyo?
→ Is Sujin in Japan now?
👉 아니요, 일본에 없어요.
Aniyo, Ilbone eopseoyo.
→ No, not in Japan.

### 💬 Useful Conversation Patterns
-에게 (to someone) for sending 
-에게서 (from someone) for receiving.

I sent gift to friend:
친구에게 선물을 보냈어요.
[chingu-ege seonmureul bonaesseoyo]

I received beautiful clock as gift from my friend:
친구에게서 예쁜 시계를 선물로 받았어요.
[chingu-egeseo yeppeun sigyereul seonmullo badasseoyo]

I came back from Korea last week:
저는 지난주에 한국에서 돌아왔어요.
[jeoneun jinanjue hangugeseo dorawasseoyo]

Make a call:
전화해요 / 전화를 걸어요.
[jeonhwahaeyo / jeonhwareul georeoyo]

Did you make a call?
전화했어요?
[jeonhwahaesseoyo?]

Did you receive a call?
전화를 받았어요?
[jeonhwareul badasseoyo?]

To be late:
늦다 [neutda]
→ to be late

늦었어요 [neujeosseoyo]
→ I'm late / I was late

Sorry for being late:
늦어서 죄송해요.
[neujeoser joesonghaeyo]
→ Sorry for being late.

늦어서 미안해요.
[neujeoser mianhaeyo]
→ Sorry for being late. (more casual)

I got late because of rain and Bangalore traffic:
비하고 방갈로르 교통 때문에 늦었어요.
[bihago banggalloleu gyotong ttaemune neujeosseoyo]

### 📝 Grammar Pattern: "Because of" - 때문에 [ttaemune]

Structure: [Noun] + 때문에

With concrete nouns:
일 때문에 바빠요. [il ttaemune bappayo] → Busy because of work.
비 때문에 못 갔어요. [bi ttaemune mot gasseoyo] → Couldn't go because of rain.
교통 때문에 늦었어요. [gyotong ttaemune neujeosseoyo] → Late because of traffic.
감기 때문에 못 왔어요. [gamgi ttaemune mot wasseoyo] → Couldn't come because of a cold.

With pronouns/demonstratives:
그것 때문에 걱정해요. [geugeot ttaemune geokjeonghaeyo] → I'm worried because of that.
이것 때문에 문제예요. [igeot ttaemune munjeyeyo] → This is a problem because of this.

### 🔤 Grammar Pattern: Ability - V + 수 있어요 [su isseoyo]

Can: V + 수 있어요 [su isseoyo]
Cannot: V + 수 없어요 [su eopseoyo]

Examples:
갈 수 있어요. [gal su isseoyo] → I can go
갈 수 없어요. [gal su eopseoyo] → I cannot go
먹을 수 있어요. [meogeul su isseoyo] → I can eat

Practice: I can read Korean but I cannot read Japanese:
한국어를 읽을 수 있어요. 그런데 일본어를 읽을 수 없어요.
[hangugeo-reul ilgeul su isseoyo. geureonde ilboneo-reul ilgeul su eopseoyo]

### ❌ Negation Patterns: 안 vs 못

안 + verb = don't/won't (by choice)
못 + verb = can't/couldn't (unable to)

Examples:
안 가요 [an gayo] → I don't go / I won't go (by choice)
못 가요 [mot gayo] → I can't go (unable to, circumstances prevent)

안 먹어요 [an meogeoyo] → I don't eat / I won't eat (by choice)
못 먹어요 [mot meogeoyo] → I can't eat (unable to, maybe full or allergic)

### 🤝 Suggestion Patterns: Shall we/Shall I

Shall we: V + ㄹ까요? [lkkayo?]
갈까요? [galkkayo?] → Shall we go?
먹을까요? [meogeulkkayo?] → Shall we eat?
볼까요? [bolkkayo?] → Shall we see/watch?

Shall I: 제가 V + ㄹ까요? [jega ... lkkayo?]
제가 할까요? [jega halkkayo?] → Shall I do it?
제가 갈까요? [jega galkkayo?] → Shall I go?
제가 사올까요? [jega saolkkayo?] → Shall I buy and bring?
