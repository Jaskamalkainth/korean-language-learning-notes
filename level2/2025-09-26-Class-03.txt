CHAPTER 2 : 취미 활동 [chwimi hwaldong] (Hobby Activities)

## TLDR - Key Points Covered:
• **Grammar**: 못 [mot] (cannot/unable to) and -아서/어서 [aseo/eoseo] (because/since)
• **Vocabulary**: Hobbies and frequency expressions
• **Dialogues**: Talking about hobbies, abilities, and reasons
• **Practice**: Expressing what you can/cannot do and why

---

## Dialogues

**Dialogue 1 - Asking about hobbies:**
민수: 유키 씨는 취미가 뭐예요? [minsu: yuki ssineun chwimiga mwoyeyo?]
     → Minsoo: What is your hobby, Yuki?
유키: 제 취미는 수영이에요. 민수 씨도 수영을 좋아해요? [yuki: je chwimineun suyeong-ieyo. minsu ssido suyeongeul joahaeyo?]
     → Yuki: My hobby is swimming. Do you like swimming, too?
민수: 아니요. 저는 수영을 못해요. [minsu: aniyo. jeoneun suyeongeul mothaeyo.]
     → Minsoo: No, I can't swim.
유키: 그러면 민수 씨는 취미가 뭐예요? [yuki: geureomyeon minsu ssineun chwimiga mwoyeyo?]
     → Yuki: Then, what is your hobby?
민수: 제 취미는 낚시예요. 주말에 바다에서 낚시를 해요. [minsu: je chwimineun naksieyo. jumare badaseo naksireul haeyo.]
     → Minsoo: My hobby is fishing. I go fishing at the sea on the weekends.

**Dialogue 2 - Talking about frequency:**
민수: 유키 씨, 수영을 자주 해요? [minsu: yuki ssi, suyeongeul jaju haeyo?]
     → Minsoo: Yuki, do you often go swimming?
유키: 아니요. 요즘은 너무 바빠서 거의 못해요. [yuki: aniyo. yojeumeun neomu bappaseo geoui mothaeyo.]
     → Yuki: No, I don't go swimming often nowadays because I've been too busy.
민수: 민수 씨는 낚시를 자주 해요? [minsu: minsu ssineun naksireul jaju haeyo?]
     → Minsoo: Do you often go fishing, Minsoo?
민수: 네. 요즘 날씨가 좋아서 자주 해요. 내일도 갈 거예요. [minsu: ne. yojeum nalssiga joaseo jaju haeyo. naeildo gal geoyeyo.]
     → Minsoo: Yes, I often go fishing because the weather's been nice recently. I'll go tomorrow, too.
유키: 저도 다음에 같이 가요. [yuki: jeodo daeume gachi gayo.]
     → Yuki: I'll go together with you next time.

## Vocabulary

**Hobbies (취미 chwimi):**
- 배드민턴을 치다 [baedeumintoneul chida] → to play badminton
- 자전거를 타다 [jajeongeoreul tada] → to ride a bike
- 등산을 하다 [deungsaneul hada] → to hike
- 낚시를 하다 [naksireul hada] → to fish
- 피아노를 치다 [pianoreul chida] → to play piano
- 게임을 하다 [geimeul hada] → to play a game

**Frequency (빈도 bindo):**
- 언제나 / 항상 [eonjena / hangsang] → always
- 자주 [jaju] → often
- 가끔 [gakkeum] → sometimes
- 거의 [geoui] → almost never
- 별로 [byeollo] → not very often/much
- 전혀 [jeonhyeo] → not at all

---

## Grammar Points

**1. 못 [mot] (cannot / unable to)**
Usage: Placed before verbs to indicate inability or circumstances preventing action
Examples:
- 저는 수영을 못해요 [jeoneun suyeongeul mothaeyo] → I can't swim
- 못 봐요. 월요일에 시험이 있어요 [mot bwayo. woryoire siheomi isseoyo] → I can't watch it. I have an exam on Monday
- 점심을 못 먹었어요 [jeomsimeul mot meogeosseoyo] → I couldn't eat lunch

**2. -아서/어서 [aseo/eoseo] (because / since)**
Usage: Attached to verb/adjective stems to show reason or cause
Examples:
- 바빠서 거의 못해요 [bappaseo geoui mothaeyo] → I almost never do it because I'm busy
- 날씨가 좋아서 자주 해요 [nalssiga joaseo jaju haeyo] → I often do it because the weather is nice
- 피곤해서 거의 안 해요 [pigonhaeseo geoui an haeyo] → I almost never do it because I'm tired

---

## Practice Examples

**Frequency + Reason patterns:**
- 자전거를 자주 타요? 날씨가 좋아서 자주 타요 [jajeongeoreul jaju tayo? nalssiga joaseo jaju tayo]
  → Do you often ride a bike? I often ride it because the weather is nice
- 피아노를 자주 쳐요? 시간이 없어서 가끔 쳐요 [pianoreul jaju chyeoyo? sigani eopseoseo gakkeum chyeoyo]
  → Do you often play piano? I play it sometimes because I don't have time
- 수영을 자주 해요? 피곤해서 거의 안 해요 [suyeongeul jaju haeyo? pigonhaeseo geoui an haeyo]
  → Do you often swim? I almost never do it because I'm tired

**Additional practice activities:**
- 운동을 하다 [undongeul hada] → exercise
- 등산을 하다 [deungsaneul hada] → hiking
- 영화를 보다 [yeonghwareul boda] → watching movies
- 요리를 하다 [yorireul hada] → cooking

## Key Patterns & Useful Phrases

**Asking about hobbies:**
- 취미가 뭐예요? [chwimiga mwoyeyo?] → What is your hobby?
- [Activity]을/를 잘해요? [[Activity]eul/reul jalhaeyo?] → Do you do [activity] well?
- [Activity]을/를 자주 해요? [[Activity]eul/reul jaju haeyo?] → Do you often do [activity]?

**Expressing inability:**
- [Activity]을/를 못해요 [mot haeyo] → I can't do [activity]
- [Activity]을/를 못 했어요 [mot haesseoyo] → I couldn't do [activity]

**Giving reasons:**
- [Reason]아서/어서 [Result] [aseo/eoseo] → because [reason], [result]
- 바빠서 [bappaseo] → because I'm busy
- 시간이 없어서 [sigani eopseoseo] → because I don't have time
- 피곤해서 [pigonhaeseo] → because I'm tired

---

## Additional Class Notes

- **못 vs 안**: 못 = can't (ability/circumstance), 안 = don't/won't (choice)
- **Frequency order**: 언제나/항상 > 자주 > 가끔 > 거의 > 별로 > 전혀
- **거의** usually used with negative forms (안/못)
- **별로, 전혀** always negative, followed by 안/못
- Practice combining frequency + reason patterns for natural conversation
