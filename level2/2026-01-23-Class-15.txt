## Dialogue 1
마크: 수진 씨의 가족은 몇 명이에요?
[Makeu: Sujin ssi-ui gajog-eun myeot myeong-i-eyo?]
→ Mark: How many family members do you have?

수진: 다섯 명이에요. 아버지, 어머니, 언니, 동생이 있어요.
[Sujin: daseot myeong-i-eyo. abeoji, eomeoni, eonni, dongsaeng-i isseoyo.]
→ Sujin: Five. I have my father, mother, older sister, and younger sibling.

마크: 남동생이에요, 여동생이에요?
[Makeu: namdongsaeng-i-eyo, yeodongsaeng-i-eyo?]
→ Mark: Is it a younger brother or sister?

수진: 여동생이에요. 마크 씨는 가족이 몇 명이에요?
[Sujin: yeodongsaeng-i-eyo. Makeu ssi-neun gajogi myeot myeong-i-eyo?]
→ Sujin: Younger sister. How many family members do you have?

마크: 우리 가족은 아버지, 어머니, 저, 세 명이에요.
[Makeu: uri gajog-eun abeoji, eomeoni, jeo, se myeong-i-eyo.]
→ Mark: I have my father, my mother, and me.

저는 형제가 없어요.
[jeo-neun hyeongje-ga eopseoyo.]
→ I don't have any brothers or sisters.

## Dialogue 2
수진: 저는 할아버지, 할머니하고 같이 살아요.
[Sujin: jeo-neun harabeoji, halmeoni-hago gachi sarayo.]
→ Sujin: I live with my grandfather and grandmother.

치엔 씨도 할아버지, 할머니하고 같이 살아요?
[Chien ssi-do harabeoji, halmeoni-hago gachi sarayo?]
→ Do you live with your grandparents too?

치엔: 아니요. 할아버지는 고향에 계세요.
[Chien: aniyo. harabeoji-neun gohyang-e gyeseyo.]
→ Chien: No. My grandfather lives in our hometown.

그리고 할머니는 돌아가셨어요.
[geurigo halmeoni-neun dora-gasyeosseoyo.]
→ And my grandmother passed away.

수진: 할아버지는 건강하세요?
[Sujin: harabeoji-neun geonganghaseyo?]
→ Sujin: Is your grandfather healthy?

치엔: 네. 운동을 자주 하셔서 아주 건강하세요.
[Chien: ne. undong-eul jaju hasyeoseo aju geonganghaseyo.]
→ Chien: Yes. He is getting healthier as he works out very often.

## Vocabulary: 가족 (Family)
- 할아버지  [harabeoji]  → grandfather
- 할머니  [halmeoni]  → grandmother
- 아버지  [abeoji]  → father
- 어머니  [eomeoni]  → mother
- 언니/누나  [eonni/nuna]  → older sister (언니: said by female / 누나: said by male)
- 오빠/형  [oppa/hyeong]  → older brother (오빠: said by female / 형: said by male)
- 동생  [dongsaeng]  → younger sibling

## Vocabulary: 존대 표현 (Honorifics)
- 계시다  [gyesida]  → to be (honorific)
- 드시다  [deusida]  → to eat (honorific)
- 주무시다  [jumusida]  → to sleep (honorific)
- 말씀하시다  [malsseumhasida]  → to say (honorific)
- 돌아가시다  [dora-gasida]  → to have passed away (honorific)
- 편찮으시다  [pyeonchaneusida]  → to be sick (honorific)

## Grammar 1: 의
[ui]
→ possessive particle (belongs to / of)

'의'는 명사에 붙어서 앞 명사의 소유임을 나타낸다.
[ui-neun myeongsa-e buteo-seo ap myeongsa-ui soyu-im-eul natanaenda.]
→ '의' is attached to a noun to indicate that this noun belongs to the noun before '의'.

### Examples:
가: 수진 씨의 동생은 학생이에요?
[ga: Sujin ssi-ui dongsaeng-eun haksaeng-i-eyo?]
→ A: Is Sujin's younger sibling a student?

나: 아니요. 회사원이에요.
[na: aniyo. hoesawon-i-eyo.]
→ B: No. (They're) an office worker.

가: 이 책은 타완 씨의 책이에요?
[ga: i chaeg-eun Tawan ssi-ui chaeg-i-eyo?]
→ A: Is this book Tawan's book?

나: 아니요. 투이 씨의 책이에요.
[na: aniyo. Tui ssi-ui chaeg-i-eyo.]
→ B: No. It's Tui's book.

### Pronunciation note:
명사 뒤의 '의'는 '에'로 발음할 수 있다.
[myeongsa dwi-ui 'ui'-neun 'e'-ro balum-hal su itda.]
→ The '의' after a noun can be pronounced as '에'.

## Grammar 2: -(으)시-
[-(eu)si-]
→ honorific suffix (raises the subject)

'-(으)시-'는 동사, 형용사, '명사+이다'의 뒤에 붙어서 그 문장의 주어를 높여 줄 때 사용한다.
[-(eu)si-neun dongsa, hyeong-yongsa, 'myeongsa+ida'-ui dwi-e buteo-seo geu munjang-ui ju-eo-reul nopyeo jul ttae sayonghanda.]
→ '-(으)시-' is attached to the root of a verb, adjective, or 'noun + 이다' to indicate honorifics.

### Examples:
아버지는 경찰이십니다. 요즘 아주 바쁘십니다.
[abeoji-neun gyeongchal-isimnida. yojeum aju ppappeusimnida.]
→ (My) father is a police officer. He is very busy these days.

어머니가 전화하셨어요.
[eomeoni-ga jeonhwa-hasyeosseoyo.]
→ (My) mother called.

가: 아버지는 나가셨어요?
[ga: abeoji-neun nagasyeosseoyo?]
→ A: Did (your) father go out?

나: 아니요. 방에서 신문을 읽으세요.
[na: aniyo. bang-eseo sinmun-eul ilgeuseyo.]
→ B: No. He's reading the newspaper in his room.

## Practice (from textbook)

### Example: asking about family
가: 치엔 씨의 형은 직업이 뭐예요?
[ga: Chien ssi-ui hyeong-eun jigeob-i mwo-yeyo?]
→ A: What is Chien's older brother's job?

나: 치엔 씨의 형은 회사원이에요.
[na: Chien ssi-ui hyeong-eun hoesawon-i-eyo.]
→ B: Chien's older brother is an office worker.

### Example: honorifics with family
가: 아버지는 무엇을 하세요?
[ga: abeoji-neun mueos-eul haseyo?]
→ A: What does (your) father do?

나: 아버지는 운동을 하세요.
[na: abeoji-neun undong-eul haseyo.]
→ B: (My) father exercises.

예: 우리 할아버지는 돌아가셨습니다. 할머니는 아주 건강하십니다.
[ye: uri harabeoji-neun dora-gasyeotseumnida. halmeoni-neun aju geonganghasimnida.]
→ e.g. My grandfather has passed away. My grandmother is very healthy.
