## Unit 1: 안부 — Saying Hello

---

### Pattern: ~고

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 고

**Meaning:** Connects two clauses on equal footing — "and / and then". Lists or joins two actions or states without implying cause or contrast.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 마크 씨는 한국 음식을 좋아하고 한국 노래도 좋아해요. | Mark likes Korean food and also likes Korean songs. |
| 오늘은 덥고 비가 와요. | Today it's hot and it's raining. |
| 아르바이트를 하고 한국에 갔다 왔어요. | I worked part-time and then went to Korea and came back. |

---

### Pattern: 그런데

**Form:** Sentence. 그런데 + contrasting sentence.

**Meaning:** "But / However / By the way" — the following sentence contrasts with or shifts from the previous one.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 네, 이번 주에는 좀 바빠요. 그런데 다음 주에는 시간이 있어요. | Yes, I'm a bit busy this week. But next week I have time. |
| 괜찮아요. 그런데 마크 씨는 언제 와요? | I'm fine. But when is Mark coming? |

## Unit 2: 취미 — Hobbies

---

### Pattern: 못

**Form:** 못 + Verb

**Meaning:** Expresses inability — you are not able to do something (due to lack of ability) or the situation prevents you from doing it.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 수영을 못해요. | I can't swim. |
| 아니요, 못 봐요. 월요일에 시험이 있어요. | No, I can't (watch it). I have a test on Monday. |

> **Note:** 못 is placed directly before the verb. Compare with 안 (which expresses a choice not to do something).

---

### Pattern: ~아/어서

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 아/어서

**Meaning:** Expresses **reason or cause** — "because / since". The first clause is the reason for the second clause.

> **Rule:** Use 아서 after stems with 아 or 오 vowels; use 어서 for all others. 하다 → 해서.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 바빠서 가끔 쳐요. | I'm busy, so I only play sometimes. |
| 아침에 늦게 일어나서 늦었어요. | I woke up late, so I was late. |

> **Note:** ~아/어서 cannot be followed by commands (-(으)세요) or suggestions (~(으)ㄹ까요). For those, use ~(으)니까 instead.

## Unit 3: 음식 — Food

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ래요

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)ㄹ래요

**Meaning:** Expresses the **speaker's intention** ("I'll do ~") or asks for the **listener's preference/intention** ("Will you do ~? / Do you want to ~?").

> **Rule:** Add 을래요 after consonant-ending stems; add ㄹ래요 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 불고기를 먹을래요. | I'll have bulgogi. |
| 뭐 먹을래요? | What would you like to eat? |
| 수진 씨, 내일 오후에 영화 볼래요? | Sujin, do you want to watch a movie tomorrow afternoon? |
| 좋아요. 그러면 두 시에 만날래요? | Good. Shall we meet at two then? |

---

### Pattern: 그러면

**Form:** Sentence. 그러면 + following sentence.

**Meaning:** "Then / In that case / If so" — the previous sentence provides the condition or premise for what follows.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 그래요? 그러면 저는 비빔밥을 먹을래요. | Is that so? Then I'll have bibimbap. |
| 좋아요. 그러면 두 시에 만날래요? | Good. Then shall we meet at two? |

---

### Pattern: 무슨

**Form:** 무슨 + Noun

**Meaning:** "What kind of ~?" — asks for a specific type or kind within a category.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 민수 씨는 무슨 음식을 자주 먹어요? | What food does Minsoo often eat? |
| 타완 씨는 무슨 운동을 해요? | What exercise does Tawan do? |

## Unit 4: 교통 — Transportation

---

### Pattern: ~이/가 걸리다

**Form:** Time expression + 이/가 걸리다

**Meaning:** Expresses how long something **takes** — "it takes (duration)".

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 택시를 타세요. 삼십 분이 걸려요. | Take a taxi. It takes thirty minutes. |
| 저는 고향에 기차를 타고 가요. 두 시간이 걸려요. | I take the train to my hometown. It takes two hours. |
| 책을 다 읽었어요. 하루가 걸렸어요. | I finished the book. It took one day. |

---

### Pattern: 에서 … 까지

**Form:** Place A + 에서 … Place B + 까지

**Meaning:** Marks the **starting point** (에서) and **destination** (까지) of travel — "from A to B".

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 집에서 회사까지 뭘 타고 가요? | How do you get from home to work? |
| 시청에서 미술관까지 두 시간이 걸려요. | From City Hall to the art museum it takes two hours. |

## Unit 5: 길 찾기 — Getting Directions

---

### Pattern: ~(으)로

**Form:** Noun + (으)로

**Meaning:** Attached to a **direction noun** to indicate movement **in that direction** — "to the right / this way / toward". Used with motion verbs (가다, 오다, 올라가다, etc.).

> **Rule:** Use 으로 after consonant-ending nouns (except ㄹ); use 로 after vowel-ending nouns or ㄹ-ending nouns.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 오른쪽으로 쭉 가세요. | Go straight to the right. |
| 이쪽으로 오세요. | Come this way. |
| 5층으로 가세요. | Go to the 5th floor. |

---

### Pattern: ~아/어서 (sequential)

**Form:** Verb stem + 아/어서 + following action

**Meaning:** Connects **sequential actions** — "and then / after doing ~". The second action follows naturally from the first.

> **Note:** This usage connects ordered steps; the subject must be the same in both clauses.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 길을 건너서 똑바로 가세요. | Cross the street and then go straight. |
| 2층으로 올라가서 왼쪽으로 가세요. | Go up to the 2nd floor and then turn left. |

## Unit 6: 전화 — Phone Calls

---

### Pattern: ~아/어 주다

**Form:** Verb stem + 아/어 주다

**Meaning:** Doing an action **for someone else's benefit** — "to do (something) for (someone)". Often used as a request with ~아/어 주세요.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 여기에 전화번호 좀 써 주세요. | Please write your phone number here (for me). |
| 펜 좀 빌려 주세요. | Please lend me a pen. |
| 친구한테 선물을 사 줬어요. | I bought a gift for my friend. |

---

### Pattern: 한테

**Form:** Noun (person) + 한테

**Meaning:** Marks the **recipient or target** of an action — "to (a person) / from (a person)". Used in informal/colloquial speech; 께 is the honorific equivalent.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 수진 씨한테 전화하세요. | Call Sujin. |
| 선생님한테 물어보세요. | Ask the teacher. |
| 친구한테 선물을 사 줬어요. | I bought a gift for my friend. |

---

### Pattern: ~지요

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 지요 (casual: ~죠)

**Meaning:** Seeks **confirmation** of something the speaker already believes to be true — "right? / isn't it? / aren't you?".

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 거기 김은주 선생님 댁이지요? | That's Teacher Kim Eun-ju's home, right? |
| 요즘에도 많이 바쁘지요? | You've been busy lately too, right? |

## Unit 7: 외모 — Appearance

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄴ (adjective modifier)

**Form:** Adjective stem + (으)ㄴ + Noun

**Meaning:** Turns an adjective into a **modifier** that describes a following noun — equivalent to "-ing" or "-ed" in English adjectives (e.g. 키가 큰 사람 = "a tall person").

> **Rule:** Add 은 after consonant-ending stems; add ㄴ after vowel-ending stems.
> **Exception:** 있다/없다 use 있는/없는 (the verb modifier form).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저기 키가 큰 사람이 수진 씨예요. | That tall person over there is Sujin. |
| 긴 머리를 좋아해요. | I like long hair. |
| 재미있는 영화를 봤어요. | I watched an interesting movie. |

---

### Pattern: ~고 있다

**Form:** Verb stem + 고 있다

**Meaning:** Expresses an **ongoing state** — "is doing ~" (present progressive). Especially used with verbs like 입다, 신다, 쓰다, 매다, 들다 (wearing/holding items) to describe a current state of dress.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 토야 씨는 흰색 치마를 입고 있어요. | Toya is wearing a white skirt. |
| 로라 씨는 검은색 가방을 들고 있어요. | Lora is carrying a black bag. |
| 마크 씨는 안경을 쓰고 넥타이를 매고 있어요. | Mark is wearing glasses and a tie. |

## Unit 8: 가족 — Family

---

### Pattern: 의 (possessive)

**Form:** Noun A + 의 + Noun B

**Meaning:** Marks **possession** — "A's B / B of A".

> **Note:** 의 can be pronounced [에] in casual speech. It is often dropped entirely in everyday conversation.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 수진 씨의 동생은 학생이에요? | Is Sujin's younger sibling a student? |
| 이 책은 타완 씨의 책이에요? | Is this book Tawan's? |

---

### Pattern: ~(으)시- (honorific)

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + (으)시 + ending

**Meaning:** Inserted into verb endings to **honor the subject** — used when talking about or to someone of higher social status (elders, teachers, etc.).

> **Common forms:** 가시다, 오시다, 드시다, 주무시다, 계시다, -(으)세요 (honorific command)

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 아버지는 경찰이십니다. 요즘 아주 바쁘십니다. | My father is a police officer. He is very busy these days. |
| 어머니가 전화하셨어요. | My mother called. |
| 아버지는 방에서 신문을 읽으세요. | Father is reading the newspaper in his room. |

## Unit 9: 여행 — Travel

---

### Pattern: ~아/어 보다

**Form:** Verb stem + 아/어 보다

**Meaning:** Expresses **having tried** or **having experienced** something — "to have tried doing ~" (when used in past tense: ~아/어 봤다). Can also suggest trying something new (imperative: ~아/어 보세요).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 제주도에 가 봤어요? | Have you ever been to Jeju Island? |
| 네, 가 봤어요. 아주 아름다워요. | Yes, I've been. It's very beautiful. |
| 어제 잡채를 만들어 봤어요. | I tried making japchae yesterday. |
| 아르바이트를 해 봤어요? | Have you ever done part-time work? |

---

### Pattern: ~고 싶다

**Form:** Verb stem + 고 싶다

**Meaning:** Expresses a **wish or desire** — "want to do ~".

> **Note:** To say someone else wants to do something, use ~고 싶어하다 (3rd person).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 놀이공원에 가고 싶어요. | I want to go to an amusement park. |
| 갈비를 먹고 싶어요. | I want to eat galbi. |
| 우리는 한국 드라마를 보고 싶어요. | We want to watch Korean dramas. |

## Unit 10: 건강 — Health

---

### Pattern: ~지 말다

**Form:** Verb stem + 지 말다 (usually: ~지 마세요 as a prohibition)

**Meaning:** Expresses **prohibition** — "don't do ~". Used to tell someone not to do something.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 말을 많이 하지 마세요. | Don't talk too much. |
| 커피를 너무 많이 마시지 마세요. | Don't drink too much coffee. |
| 박물관 안에서 사진을 찍지 마세요. | Don't take pictures inside the museum. |

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ 후에

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)ㄹ 후에 / Noun + 후에

**Meaning:** "After (doing) ~" — the action in the second clause happens after the first.

> **Rule:** Add 을 후에 after consonant-ending stems; add ㄹ 후에 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 한 시간 일한 후에 10분 쉬어 보세요. | After working one hour, try resting for 10 minutes. |
| 저녁을 먹은 후에 공원에서 산책을 했어요. | After eating dinner, I took a walk in the park. |
| 그 책을 다 읽은 후에 저도 빌려 주세요. | After you finish reading that book, lend it to me too. |
| 10분 후에 다시 전화해 보세요. | Try calling again in 10 minutes. |

## Unit 11: 모임 — Gatherings

---

### Pattern: ~아/어야 하다 / ~아/어야 되다

**Form:** Verb stem + 아/어야 하다 (or 되다)

**Meaning:** Expresses **obligation or necessity** — "have to / must / should do ~".

> **Note:** ~아/어야 하다 and ~아/어야 되다 are interchangeable in most contexts.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 청소를 하고 음식을 만들어야 해요. | I have to clean and make food. |
| 빨리 가야 해요. | I have to go quickly. |
| 공부해야 돼요. | I have to study. |

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ게요

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)ㄹ게요

**Meaning:** Expresses the **speaker's intention or promise** in response to a situation — "I'll do ~". Implies awareness of the other person's situation or expectation.

> **Rule:** Add 을게요 after consonant-ending stems; add ㄹ게요 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 그러면 제가 지금 연락할게요. | Then I'll contact her now. |
| 네, 내일 갈게요. | Yes, I'll come tomorrow. |

## Unit 12: 고향 — Hometowns

---

### Pattern: ~지만

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + 지만 (Noun + 이지만)

**Meaning:** **Contrast** between two clauses — "but / however / although". Acknowledges the first clause and contrasts it with the second.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 여름에는 덥지만 겨울에는 추워요. | It's hot in summer, but cold in winter. |
| 시골은 경치는 좋지만 불편해요. | The countryside has nice scenery, but it's inconvenient. |
| 민수 씨는 운동을 좋아하지만 수진 씨는 안 좋아해요. | Minsoo likes exercise, but Sujin doesn't. |

---

### Pattern: 보다 (comparison)

**Form:** Noun A + 보다 (+ more/less adjective)

**Meaning:** **Comparative** — "more than / compared to A". The noun before 보다 is the standard of comparison.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 제 고향은 여기보다 작아요. | My hometown is smaller than here. |
| 제주도가 서울보다 공기가 맑아요. | Jeju's air is cleaner than Seoul's. |
| 동생이 저보다 운동을 잘해요. | My younger sibling is better at sports than me. |

## Unit 13: 기분과 감정 — Feelings and Emotions

---

### Pattern: ~(으)ㄹ 때

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + (으)ㄹ 때 / Noun + 일 때

**Meaning:** "When ~ / while ~" — describes **the time or circumstance** in which something happens.

> **Rule:** Add 을 때 after consonant-ending stems; add ㄹ 때 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 기분이 안 좋을 때 저는 운동을 해요. | When I feel bad, I exercise. |
| 슬플 때 음악을 들어요. | When I'm sad, I listen to music. |

---

### Pattern: ~(으)러 가다/오다

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)러 가다 / 오다

**Meaning:** Expresses **purpose of movement** — "go/come (somewhere) in order to do ~".

> **Rule:** Add 으러 after consonant-ending stems; add 러 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 기분 전환을 하러 갈까요? | Shall we go to refresh ourselves? |
| 저는 공원에 사진을 찍으러 가요. | I go to the park to take pictures. |
| 유키 씨는 친구를 만나러 시내에 갔어요. | Yuki went downtown to meet a friend. |

---

## Unit 14: 미래 — Future Plans

---

### Pattern: ~(으)려고

**Form:** Verb stem + (으)려고

**Meaning:** Expresses **purpose or intention** — "in order to do ~ / planning to do ~". The subject performs an action with the intention stated in the second clause.

> **Rule:** Add 으려고 after consonant-ending stems; add 려고 after vowel-ending stems.
> **Note:** The subject must be the same in both clauses. Cannot be used with ~(으)세요 or ~(으)ㄹ까요 endings.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 한국어 번역가가 되려고 한국어를 공부해요. | I'm studying Korean in order to become a Korean translator. |
| 친구를 만나려고 밖으로 나갔어요. | I went outside to meet a friend. |
| 책을 읽으려고 책상에 앉았어요. | I sat at my desk in order to read a book. |

---

### Pattern: ~(으)면

**Form:** Verb/Adjective stem + (으)면 / Noun + (이)면

**Meaning:** Expresses a **condition or supposition** — "if ~ / when ~". The second clause is the result or response to the condition in the first clause.

> **Rule:** Add 으면 after consonant-ending stems; add 면 after vowel-ending stems.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 대학교를 졸업하면 한국 회사에 취직하고 싶어요. | When I graduate from university, I want to get a job at a Korean company. |
| 주말에 날씨가 좋으면 같이 공원에 갈래요? | If the weather is good this weekend, shall we go to the park together? |
