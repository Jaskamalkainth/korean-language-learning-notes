## Time Expressions (시간 표현 - Sigan Pyohyeon)

### AM/PM
오전 [ojeon] - AM
오후 [ohu] - PM

### Time Format
시간 [sigan] - hour
분 [bun] - minute

### Examples:
3:05 PM → 오후 세 시 오 분 [ohu se si o bun]
9:30 AM → 오전 아홉 시 삼십 분 [ojeon ahop si samsip bun]
12:00 PM → 오후 열두 시 [ohu yeoldu si]
11:45 AM → 오전 열한 시 사십오 분 [ojeon yeolhan si sasibo bun]

### Notes:
- Use native Korean numbers (하나, 둘, 셋...) for hours
- Use Sino-Korean numbers (일, 이, 삼...) for minutes
- 시 [si] is used after the hour
- 분 [bun] is used after the minutes
- When it's exactly on the hour, 분 is omitted
  Example: 3:00 PM → 오후 세 시 [ohu se si]

CHAPTER - 6

어제 일과 (Yesterday's Activities) [eoje ilgwa]

Dialogue 1:
A: 유키 씨는 어제 뭐 했어요? [yuki ssineun eoje mwo haesseoyo?]
   → Yuki, what did you do yesterday?
B: 민수 씨를 만났어요. 우리는 같이 밥을 먹었어요. [minsu ssireul mannasseoyo. urineun gachi babeul meogeosseoyo.]
   → I met Minsoo. We ate together.
A: 집에 있었어요? [jibe isseosseoyo?]
   → Were you at home?
B: 네, 집에 있었어요. [ne, jibe isseosseoyo.]
   → Yes, I stayed at home.

Dialogue 2:
A: 마크 씨, 어제 뭐 했어요? [makeu ssi, eoje mwo haesseoyo?]
   → Mark, what did you do yesterday?
B: 한국 영화를 봤어요. [hanguk yeonghwareul bwasseoyo.]
   → I watched a Korean movie.
A: 영화관에서 영화를 봤어요? [yeonghwagwaneseo yeonghwareul bwasseoyo?]
   → Did you watch it in the theater?
B: 아니요. 집에서 봤어요. [aniyo. jibeseo bwasseoyo.]
   → No, I watched it at home.

## Vocabulary

## Grammar Points

1. -았어요 / -었어요 [-asseoyo / -eosseoyo] (Past tense marker)
Used after verbs/adjectives to indicate past tense.
Examples:
- 갔어요 [gasseoyo] → went
- 만났어요 [mannasseoyo] → met
- 먹었어요 [meogeosseoyo] → ate
- 공부했어요 [gongbuhaesseoyo] → studied

2. 에서 [eseo] (Location marker)
Indicates the place where an action occurs.
Examples:
- 도서관에서 책을 읽었어요 [doseogwaneseo chaegeul ilgeosseoyo] → I read a book at the library
- 카페에서 커피를 마셨어요 [kapeseo keopireul masyeosseoyo] → I drank coffee at the café

## Practice Examples
- 어제 친구를 만났어요. [eoje chingureul mannasseoyo.] — I met a friend yesterday.
- 공원에서 운동했어요. [gongwoneseo undonghasseoyo.] — I exercised at the park.
- 영화관에서 영화를 봤어요. [yeonghwagwaneseo yeonghwareul bwasseoyo.] → I watched a movie at the theater.
- 집에서 쉬었어요. [jibeseo swiesseoyo.] → I rested at home.

## Useful Questions
- 어제 뭐 했어요? [eoje mwo haesseoyo?] → What did you do yesterday?
- 어디에서 밥을 먹었어요? [eodieseo babeul meogeosseoyo?] → Where did you eat?
- 영화관에서 봤어요? [yeonghwagwaneseo bwasseoyo?] → Did you see it at the theater?
 
Verbs (동사):
- 보다 [boda] → to see | Present: 봐요 [bwayo] | Past: 봤어요 [bwasseoyo]
- 읽다 [ikda] → to read | Present: 읽어요 [ilgeoyo] | Past: 읽었어요 [ilgeosseoyo]
- 듣다 [deutda] → to listen | Present: 들어요 [deureoyo] | Past: 들었어요 [deureosseoyo]
- 마시다 [masida] → to drink | Present: 마셔요 [masyeoyo] | Past: 마셨어요 [masyeosseoyo]
- 만나다 [mannada] → to meet | Present: 만나요 [mannayo] | Past: 만났어요 [mannasseoyo]
- 앉다 [anda] → to sit | Present: 앉아요 [anjayo] | Past: 앉았어요 [anjasseoyo]
- 놀다 [nolda] → to play | Present: 놀아요 [norayo] | Past: 놀았어요 [norasseoyo]
- 기다리다 [gidarida] → to wait | Present: 기다려요 [gidaryeoyo] | Past: 기다렸어요 [gidaryeosseoyo]
- 일하다 [ilhada] → to work | Present: 일해요 [ilhaeyo] | Past: 일했어요 [ilhaesseoyo]

- 쉬다 [swida] → to rest | Present: 쉬어요 [swieoyo] | Past: 쉬었어요 [swiesseoyo]

## Location Markers
- 에 [e] → to (destination)
- 에서 [eseo] → at/in (location where action occurs)

Examples:
- 학교에 가요 [hakgyoe gayo] → I go to school
- 학교에서 공부해요 [hakgyoeseo gongbuhaeyo] → I study at school
- 집에 있어요 [jibe isseoyo] → I am at home
- 집에서 쉬어요 [jibeseo swieoyo] → I rest at home

## Transportation Vocabulary & Culture (교통 - Gyotong)

### Public Transportation Types
- 버스 [beoseu] → bus
- 지하철 [jihacheol] → subway
- 택시 [taeksi] → taxi
- KTX [keiteekseu] → Korea's high-speed rail

### Common Phrases
- 버스를 타요 [beoseureul tayo] → I take the bus
- 지하철을 타요 [jihacheoreul tayo] → I take the subway
- 택시를 불렀어요 [taeksireul bulleosseoyo] → I called a taxi

### Transportation Places
- 버스 정류장 [beoseu jeongryujang] → bus stop
- 버스 터미널 [beoseu teomineol] → bus terminal
- 지하철역 [jihacheoryeok] → subway station
- 택시 승강장 [taeksi seunggjangjang] → taxi stand

### Transportation Cards
- 교통 카드 [gyotong kadeu] → transportation card
- 충전기 [chungjeongi] → card recharger

### Major Cities with Subway Systems
- 서울 [seoul] → Seoul
- 부산 [busan] → Busan
- 대구 [daegu] → Daegu
- 광주 [gwangju] → Gwangju
- 대전 [daejeon] → Daejeon
- 인천 [incheon] → Incheon

### Cultural Notes
- Public transportation in Korea is highly efficient and widely used
- Transportation cards can be used across multiple cities and different types of transport
- Subway announcements are made in Korean, English, and sometimes Chinese/Japanese
- Subway stations often announce: "이번 역은 [station name]입니다" [ibeon yeogeun ... imnida] → "This station is..."
- "다음 역은 [station name]입니다" [daeum yeogeun ... imnida] → "The next station is..."

### Additional Station Announcements
- "이번 정류장은 [station name]입니다" [ibeon jeongryujangeun ... imnida] → "This stop is..."
- "다음 정류장은 [station name]입니다" [daeum jeongryujangeun ... imnida] → "The next stop is..."

### Important Stations
- 용산역 [yongsan-yeok] → Yongsan Station
- 명동역 [myeongdong-yeok] → Myeongdong Station
- 이대역 [idae-yeok] → Ewha Womans University Station

### Additional Transportation Phrases
- "여기 앞에 택시 타는 곳이 있어요" [yeogi ape taeksi taneun gosi isseoyo] → "There's a taxi stand in front here"
- "저는 KTX 타고 서울역에 가요" [jeoneun keiteekseu tago seouryeoge gayo] → "I'm taking the KTX to Seoul Station"

### Proverb
- 천리 길도 한 걸음부터 [cheolli gildo han georeumbuteo] → "Even a thousand-mile journey begins with a single step"
  - 천리 [cheolli] - thousand miles (천 = thousand, 리 = Korean mile)
  - 길 [gil] - road/path
  - 도 [do] - also/even
  - 한 [han] - one
  - 걸음 [georeum] - step
  - 부터 [buteo] - from/starting with

- "A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step."
